PUBLIC NOTICE
RULES AND REGULATIONS
OF THE FIFTY-FIFTY SIDEWALK
REPAIR PILOT PROGRAM
DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC SERVICE
These Rules and Regulations are
issued pursuant to Section 6 of Ord.
No. 725-02, passed by the Council of
the City on June, 10, 2002. These

The City Record

July 17, 2002
Rules and Regulations shall take
effect immediately upon publication
in the City Record.
1. The Program shall be available
on a first-come, first-serve basis to
eligible residents. To be eligible for
the Program, an individual must
reside in a ward for which Neighborhood Equity Funds are certified
and available for this purpose. The
sidewalks to be repaired must abut
the individual’s principal residence.
2. The sidewalks must be determined to be in an unsafe or dangerous condition in the opinion of
the Director of Public Service.
3. If the resident is eligible under
Paragraph 1, and if the sidewalks
meet the condition under Paragraph
2, then the resident may participate
in the Program by paying fifty percent of the estimated costs to be
shared
to
the
Commissioner
of
Assessments
and
Licenses
and
acknowledging
in
writing
these
Rules and Regulations and the estimated shared costs.
4. All work performed under the
Program shall be performed by the
City’s Contractor, under the direction and control of the Department
of Public Service.
The City shall
not be liable for any claims arising
during performance of the work or
after the work has been performed.
5. The resident must not act in a
manner that will interfere with the
City’s contractor and must cooperate
fully with any reasonable request
by the City or its contractor in connection with performance of the
work.
6. If the resident’s portion of the
shared cost exceeds the estimated
payment,
the
Commissioner
of
Assessments
and
Licenses
shall
invoice the owner for the additional
payment, which payment shall be
due within thirty (30) days of the
date of the invoice. If the resident’s
portion of the shared cost is less
than the estimated payment, the
Commissioner of Assessments and
Licenses shall refund the overpayment within thirty (30) days after
receipt of actual cost information
from the Department of Public Service.
7. If requested by the City, the resident shall produce proof of residency, such as a voter registration
card, utility bills, etc.
8. If, at any time during the oneyear period following the date of
completion of the work, the resident finds any defects, omissions or
inadequacies for any cause which
may be attributed to the City’s contractor, the resident must promptly
notify the Director of Public Service in writing. Upon such notification, the Director or his designee
shall determine if a defect or omission or inadequacy exists, which
determination shall be binding on
the resident. If there is a defect,
omission or inadequacy, the Director shall demand that the City’s
contractor correct it.
9. These Rules and Regulations
may be amended upon publication of
amended Rules and Regulations in
the City Record.

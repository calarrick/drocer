a municipal department; now, therefore,

the approval stated in Section 4

Section 392.01 Definitions
As used in this chapter:
(a) “Hearing impaired” means a
hearing problem impairing the ability to be awakened by a standard
smoke detector or carbon monoxide
alarm whether or not wearing a hearing aid.
(b) “Occupant” means a person over
one year of age, living, sleeping,
cooking or eating in, or having actual
possession of a dwelling unit or room
used for room occupancy.
(c) “Owner” means the owner or
owners of the premises, a vendee in

The City Record
possession, a mortgagee or receiver
in possession, a lessee or joint lessees
of the whole thereof, or an agent as
any other person, firm or corporation
directly in control of the premises or
having a legal or equitable interest in
the property.
(d) “Sleeping area” means an area
of a dwelling containing bedrooms
which are separated from each other
by no use area other than a bathroom.
(e) “Smoke detector” means an
approved device which detects visible
or invisible particles of combustion
and which emits an audible signal
indicating a fire condition or both an
audible and a visual signal indicating
a fire condition.
(f) “Carbon monoxide alarm”
means an approved device which
detects the invisible gas known as
carbon monoxide and which emits an
audible signal indicating the presence of unhealthy or lethal levels of
carbon monoxide, or both an audible
and a visual signal indicating the
presence of unhealthy or lethal levels
of carbon monoxide.
Section 392.02 Smoke Detectors Installation Required
(a) Owners of each new and existing dwelling unit shall install a minimum of one (1) smoke detector adjacent to the sleeping area in each
dwelling unit. At least one additional
smoke detector shall be installed on
each additional story of the dwelling
including the basement, but excluding any crawl space or unfinished
attic. For a one, two or three family
dwelling with one or more split levels,
a smoke detector on an upper level
shall suffice for an adjacent lower
level provided the lower level is less
than one full story below the upper
level. If a door intervenes between
adjacent levels, a smoke detector
shall be installed on both levels.
Smoke detectors shall be Underwriters Laboratories listed and shall be
installed in a manner and location
consistent with the manufacturer’s
instructions. Where a dwelling is
occupied by a person who is deaf or
hearing impaired and a written
request for the installation of a smoke
detector is received by the owner
from an occupant in the dwelling, a
smoke detector shall be installed by
the owner which provides a visual or
vibratile signal sufficient to warn the
deaf or hearing impaired individual
when activated.
(b) Smoke detectors and carbon
monoxide alarms in new construction
shall be installed as required by the
Building
Code.
Battery-operated
smoke detectors and carbon monoxide alarms may be installed in existing dwelling units. When alterations
or additions requiring a permit occur,
or when one or more sleeping rooms
are added or created in existing buildings, smoke detectors and carbon
monoxide alarms shall be installed in
the entire building in accordance
with the Building Code.
Section 392.03 Testing and Inspection
(a) Testing and inspection of
smoke detectors and carbon monoxide alarm in each dwelling unit shall
be performed by the occupant not less
than once a week. The owner shall
provide the occupant with printed
information as to proper testing of the
smoke detectors and carbon monoxide alarms within the dwelling.

544

April 23, 2014
(b) At every change of occupancy
of each dwelling unit occasioned by
or incidental to a sale, lease or sublease of such dwelling, it shall be the
duty of the owner to inspect the
smoke detectors and carbon monoxide alarms, and certify before occupancy to the new occupant that they
are in proper working condition. This
section shall not be construed to vitiate or render void any contract, lease
or sublease subject hereto. In addition to other requirements of this
chapter regarding testing and inspection of smoke detectors and carbon
monoxide alarms, each owner of a
dwelling unit shall inspect and test
each smoke detector and carbon
monoxide alarm in the dwelling unit
at least annually.
Section 392.04 Maintenance
(a) The occupant of each dwelling
unit shall notify the owner in writing
that a smoke detector or carbon
monoxide alarm is in need of replacement or repair. The owner shall
repair or replace the smoke detector
or carbon monoxide alarm within seventy-two (72) hours of receipt of the
occupant’s written notice.
(b) The owner of each dwelling
unit shall provide new batteries for
battery-operated smoke detectors and
carbon monoxide alarms at least
annually and at the beginning of any
new lease. It shall be the responsibility of the occupant to ensure that the
batteries installed are not removed.
Section 392.05 Tampering
Anyone tampering or interfering
with the effectiveness of a smoke
detector or carbon monoxide alarm
shall be in violation of this chapter
and subject to the penalties set forth
in Section 392.99.
Section 392.06 Enforcement
(a) The Chief of Fire and the Commissioner of Building and Housing
shall have concurrent jurisdiction to
conduct inspections to determine compliance with this chapter and to
enforce its provisions.
(b) Any notice of violation issued to
enforce the requirements of this chapter shall require compliance with its
provisions within seventy-two (72)
hours of the issuance of the notice. If
a notice of violation is issued for a
violation discovered subsequent to a
fire or carbon monoxide gas leak and
the dwelling unit was rendered uninhabitable by the fire or the carbon
monoxide gas leak, then the notice
shall require compliance with the provisions of this chapter prior to the
reoccupancy of the dwelling unit.
(c) No owner or occupant shall
refuse to allow inspection at reasonable times of any dwelling unit by a
duly authorized City inspector.
(d) The means of enforcement
authorized by this section shall be in
addition to and not in lieu of any other
means of enforcement that may be
authorized by these Codified Ordinances or by statute.

in these Codified Ordinances, the
above listed minor misdemeanors
may be enforced by the issuance of a

Section 392.021 Carbon Monoxide
